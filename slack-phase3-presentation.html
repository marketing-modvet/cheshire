Vet Alliance — Slack Structure

A clear home for every conversation.
May 2026 · Prepared by the Marketing department for company rollout

Vet Alliance is moving company communication onto Slack. Today, work conversations are scattered across WhatsApp groups, emails, DMs, and ad-hoc chats. That makes things slow, easy to miss, and impossible to search later.

This document explains how we're organising Slack so every message has an obvious home, every team has the channels they need, and nothing falls between the cracks. Privacy is a first-class design goal: sensitive functions stay isolated, while operational ones are visible enough to help everyone do their job.

It's designed to scale to ~200 staff across 11 clinics + Head Office, while staying simple enough that a new joiner can find their way around on day one.

The big idea

Six channel families

Every channel name starts with one of six prefixes. The prefix tells you instantly what kind of channel it is.

One owner per channel

Every channel has a clear holder who triages messages, keeps it tidy, and answers questions.

Lists, not chat, for tasks

Real work lives in lists attached to channels. Three privacy tiers depending on what the data is.

How a clinic location works

Today, every clinic shares one giant chat where vets, nurses, reception, and assistants are all mixed together. Important medical messages get lost in front-desk chatter, and vice versa.

Each location now gets 5 dedicated channels. Branch staff see only their own location's channels — they don't see other branches. The same dept head holds each function across every location, so they're members of all 12 branches' relevant channels and can spot patterns across clinics.

Lists — where work actually lives

Channels are for conversation. Lists are where work is tracked. Each functional channel has a list with task / assignee / status / due / notes columns, plus function-specific extras.

Almost everything is dept-private. Two patterns based on whether branches need their own private slice:

Single dept list Dept + management only

One list in the dept's private team channel. Only that dept + management see it. Used where there's no per-branch privacy concern.

  • Operations — maintenance, inventory, purchasing, approvals, fitout, licensing
  • Clinical — cases, product evaluations, rescue
  • CX — complaints, scheduling decisions
  • BizDev — pipeline, partnerships, expansion
  • Marketing — campaigns, content calendar
  • IT — tickets, projects
  • Project tracker (cross-team)

Per-clinic + dept master Branch isolation

Sensitive data. Branch must not see other branch; staff must not see dept-level. Per-clinic list + dept master list with sync.

  • Finance — AP, AR, discounts, pricing
  • HR — recruitment, payroll, visa
  • Per-clinic reception/FD customer issues

Worked example: a broken AC at JLT (single dept list)

  1. JLT reception notices the AC is broken in the waiting room.
  2. They post in clinic-mv-jlt-maintenance (their own private branch channel) and click "Add to list".
  3. The item lands in the maintenance team's private list inside team-ops-maintenance, tagged Branch = JLT.
  4. Only Judit + her maintenance team + management see the list. Other branches don't see it.
  5. JLT sees status updates inside their own branch channel via the dept-head's reply / cross-post.
  6. Cross-clinic patterns (3 branches with HVAC issues this week) are visible to Judit so she can negotiate with the vendor in bulk.

Worked example: customer billing query at JLT (Tier 3 — strictly private)

  1. JLT reception logs a billing query in their own private FD list (in clinic-mv-jlt-fd).
  2. The item lives only in JLT's list. Other branches do NOT see it.
  3. The item also syncs into Aida's master CX list (in her private team channel) and Harshita's finance audit list — but no other branch's staff sees JLT's customer details.
  4. Aida + Harshita coordinate the resolution. The branch sees status updates inside their own channel.
