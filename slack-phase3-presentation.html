Vet Alliance — Slack Structure

A clear home for every conversation.
May 2026 · Prepared by the Marketing department for company rollout

Vet Alliance is moving company communication onto Slack. Today, work conversations are scattered across WhatsApp groups, emails, DMs, and ad-hoc chats. That makes things slow, easy to miss, and impossible to search later.

This document explains how we're organising Slack so every message has an obvious home, every team has the channels they need, and nothing falls between the cracks. Privacy is a first-class design goal: branches see only their own channels, dept heads see across all branches, finance and HR are isolated from clinical chatter, and so on.

It's designed to scale to ~200 staff across 11 clinics + Head Office, while staying simple enough that a new joiner can find their way around on day one.

The big idea

Six channel families

Every channel name starts with one of six prefixes. The prefix tells you instantly what kind of channel it is.

One owner per channel

Every channel has a clear holder who triages messages, keeps it tidy, and answers questions.

Master Lists for tasks

Real work lives in lists, not chat. One master list per function. Branches see only their slice.

How a clinic location works

Today, every clinic shares one giant chat where vets, nurses, reception, and assistants are all mixed together. Important medical messages get lost in front-desk chatter, and vice versa.

Each location now gets 5 dedicated channels, all private to that branch. JLT staff see only JLT channels. Downtown sees only Downtown. The same dept head holds each function across every location — they're members of all 12 branches' relevant channels, while branch staff see only their own.

Lists — where work actually lives

Channels are for conversation. Lists are where work is tracked. Each functional channel has a list with task / assignee / status / due / notes columns, plus function-specific extras (Maintenance adds severity + vendor; Inventory adds supplier + status; etc.).

To respect branch privacy, we use a dual-list pattern:

Per-clinic list

Each clinic sub-channel has its own list. Only branch staff + the relevant dept head can see it.

JLT staff see only JLT's tickets, Downtown sees only Downtown's, etc.

Master aggregator (dept head's private channel)

The dept head also has a master list in their own private team channel — populated automatically from every branch list, tagged with branch.

Judit has one master Maintenance list with items from all 12 branches — no other branches see it.

Worked example: a broken AC at JLT

  1. JLT reception notices the AC is broken in the waiting room.
  2. They post in clinic-mv-jlt-maintenance and click "Add to list".
  3. The item appears in JLT's local maintenance list. Only JLT staff and Judit see it. Downtown, Palm, MB — don't.
  4. Within minutes, the item also appears in Judit's master Maintenance list (in her private dept channel), tagged Branch = JLT.
  5. Judit assigns severity, vendor, status. Updates flow back to JLT's local list.
  6. JLT sees the update right inside their own channel — without seeing anything from other branches.

Why this matters

Real privacy. JLT staff can't see Downtown's maintenance backlog. Reception staff at MB can't see VV's bookings. Branches stay focused on their own work.

Cross-clinic patterns (3 branches with HVAC issues this week → negotiate with same vendor in bulk) are visible to the dept head only — which is exactly where that decision belongs.

How teams talk to each other

Slack only works if people know where to send asks. Four mechanisms wire the structure together:

1. The "front door" — help-*

Anyone in the company can post in a help channel. The dept user-group monitors and triages — same business day response.

Example: a vet needs a poster reprinted → help-marketing → Andrew picks it up.

2. Direct dept ↔ dept channels

For recurring patterns (Call Centre ↔ Reception, branch ↔ inventory), depts use shared channels directly — no help-* middleman.

Example: team-cc-handoffs bridges Call Centre and reception leads.

3. Strategic decisions — va-management

Anything affecting 3+ departments or needing leadership air-cover goes here. All team leads see it. Decisions get logged in a pinned canvas.

4. Read-only — va-announcements

Outcomes, policy changes, big news. Posting locked to 9 leaders. Everyone reads.

The "Where do I post this?" cheat sheet

Is it a question/request to another dept? ├─ Yes, recurring pattern (CC↔reception, branch↔inventory) → direct channel └─ Yes, one-off → the relevant help-* Is it a decision that needs 3+ depts or leadership? → va-management Is it news everyone needs to know? → va-announcements (with author from authorised posters) Is it just within my team? → team-* sub-channel or clinic-XXX-* sub-channel Is it not work? → social-* (random / pets / celebrations / marketplace)
