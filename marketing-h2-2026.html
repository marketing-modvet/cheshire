06 · The honest part

Where we are constrained.

Naming the friction is part of owning the result. Three structural challenges are actively shaping the H2 2026 plan — and each one comes with a defined mitigation already in motion.

Structural · Brand portfolio breadth

Seven brands operating from a single team.

MV, BOV, VV, TVRC, BSPR, Floof and VA each require their own brand voice, social cadence, point-of-sale collateral, paid-media tracking and campaign calendar. The same headcount that historically produced output for one brand is now distributed across seven — with each brand at a different stage of maturity and demanding different mixes of effort.

Mitigation in plan: shared production engine with brand-specific briefs · sequenced campaign calendar (rather than parallel rollouts) · reusable design-system & template library · clear prioritisation tiers per brand based on revenue contribution.
Perception · Per-channel dilution

Aggregate output is large; per-channel cadence reads as modest.

500+ posts and 8,000+ photographs are substantial in aggregate, but distributed across 40 social profiles and 7 brands, the per-channel posting cadence appears modest at first glance. The output is there — the breadth of the surface area hides it. This affects how the work is perceived both internally and against external benchmarks.

Mitigation in plan: consolidated cross-brand reporting (this dashboard) · per-brand performance dashboards with cadence vs benchmark · output-per-FTE benchmarking against comparable veterinary marketing teams · regular share-of-voice reporting.
External · Google AI Overviews

Generative search is eroding blog and long-tail organic traffic.

Google's AI Overviews and similar generative-answer features are absorbing a measurable share of clicks that previously reached the Health Library and other long-tail content. This is an industry-wide shift, not Modern Vet specific — but it directly impacts our organic acquisition channel and the ROI of past content investment.

Mitigation in plan: double down on EEAT depth and doctor-attributed expert content (not commodity answers) · build YouTube as a parallel discovery channel less exposed to the same algorithm risk · invest in brand-search and direct-traffic acquisition · expand partnership-driven referral traffic.
Internal · Cross-departmental dependencies

Marketing outcomes increasingly depend on other departments.

Several of the highest-impact initiatives in the H2 plan — booking-platform bridge, ezyVet integration, attribution unification, website security transition — require coordinated execution with Tech, Clinical and Operations. Marketing can scope and design, but cannot ship alone.

Mitigation in plan: formal cross-functional working groups for each shared initiative · documented dependencies inside the H2 plan · regular standing reviews with department heads · explicit escalation path for blocked items.
Capability · Onboarding & training load

New hires arrive without veterinary or niche knowledge.

To control headcount cost, recent hires have been brought in at junior or generalist level rather than as veterinary-marketing specialists. The trade-off is significant onboarding and training load — both general marketing skill-building and Modern-Vet-specific clinical context. Output ramp-up is slower than it would be with senior, in-niche hires.

Mitigation in plan: Vet Alliance Academy as a structured internal training programme · documented playbooks per function · AI agents acting as on-demand subject-matter coaches · senior team members tasked with mentorship time.
Operational · Cross-department project load

Marketing carries meaningful project load for other departments.

Not strictly a problem — it's how an integrated company should work — but worth naming for capacity planning. Marketing actively participates in projects led by Tech, Clinical, CX, Ops and HR, contributing creative, content, comms and design work that doesn't appear in marketing's own project list.

Mitigation in plan: visible accounting of cross-department contributions in the maintenance view · service-level guidelines for inbound requests · explicit prioritisation against marketing's own initiatives.
Historic · Data tracking gaps

End-to-end attribution has been incomplete.

Marketing reporting and ROI analysis have historically been limited by gaps in the data-tracking stack — inconsistent UTM discipline, partial call-tracking coverage, fragmented booking attribution and incomplete cross-domain measurement. Decisions about where to invest paid-media budget have been made on imperfect information.

Mitigation in plan: the tracking-architecture upgrade with R&N Analytics is the headline H2 dependency · expected to provide reliable end-to-end attribution by mid-H2 · paid-media budget recommendations from Q3 onward will reflect validated data, not estimates.

08 · The Marketing Director

Scope, reality and case for review.

A clear summary of how the position has evolved — from the focused remit originally agreed at hire to the full-stack marketing leadership role it has become — followed by the case for a corresponding compensation review.

Then · Original agreement

Media Marketing Director

Hired to lead the company's media, social-media and offline output. A focused content remit:

  • Lead a content team of 3 – 5 people
  • All in-house media production — photography, video, design
  • Social-media presence and editorial direction
  • Printables, collateral and offline brand surfaces
  • Events — planning, execution and on-site activation
  • Cooperations and partnerships
  • Studio and equipment ownership
  • Marketing strategy and exploratory planning
  • Roughly the visual, social and field-marketing surface of the company.
Now · Reality

Marketing Director · full stack

The original remit is still in place in full. Around it, the role has absorbed digital, performance, eCommerce, analytics, AI and a meaningful share of finance — organically and through transferred ownership.

  • Direct leadership of a 10+ person marketing organisation — hiring, mentoring, training, quality control across content, design, paid, SEO, ops, dev
  • Strategic ownership of all performance marketing — Google Ads and paid social — budget, mix and creative direction
  • Roadmap ownership across eight brand websites, working alongside the Tech department on execution
  • Floof commercial coordination — product selection, growth initiatives, and coordination of the ECrea8 team building the next phase
  • Sole owner of brand identity across all seven group brands
  • Owner of the marketing analytics layer within the company-wide Power-BI environment, in partnership with R&N Analytics
  • Owner of SEO, paid social budget and the Google reviews programme across all brands
  • PR and events — external presence, partnerships, advocacy
  • Active contribution to customer journey and retention — working alongside CX, Operations and other departments on touchpoints, retention programmes and data-driven growth
  • Brand compliance in automated and AI-generated communications — the new front line as scale grows
  • Marketing finance — 100+ supplier invoices per month processed personally, monthly financial reporting, the project-budget tracker that this very document is built on
  • AI & automation portfolio — 25+ deployed projects → designed, built and shipped largely in personal time
  • Operationally available 12+ hours per day, including weekends, as the de-facto on-call point for marketing, brand and digital incidents
  • In effect: a full Marketing Director role covering brand, digital, eCommerce, performance, analytics, AI and marketing finance — on top of the original media leadership remit.

The case for compensation review

Six considerations — framed as forward-looking investment in a leader carrying expanded scope, not as a retrospective request for past work. Each is presented as a return the company captures, not a personal want.

01 · Scope alignment

Compensation that matches the role being performed.

The position has expanded substantially over the past year — from a focused media leadership remit into full-stack marketing direction across digital, eCommerce, analytics, AI and marketing finance. Aligning compensation with the actual scope of the role is what converts a heavy temporary load into a durable, well-led function the company can rely on for the next several years.

02 · Sustained intensity

Underwriting a 12-hour-day, 7-day operating pattern.

The current cadence — 12+ hour working days, weekend availability, additional projects shipped in personal time — is what has carried the department through this scope expansion. It produces results, but only if the human running it remains rested, healthy and motivated. Compensation that funds proper rest, healthcare and wellbeing is the structural support that keeps that intensity productive rather than fragile.

03 · Cost leverage already delivered

A leader who reduces total spend at this level pays for the increase.

Marketing spend has been actively engineered downward against revenue — from a baseline of AED 264K/month average to a planned AED 240K at 3% of an AED 8M run-rate, with structural reductions on agency contracts, tooling stack and roles transitioning to project-based engagement. The compensation review is absorbed inside an envelope that is itself shrinking, not breaking it.

04 · Major operating savings ahead

WhatsApp + Veta AI bot · call-centre cost deflection.

One of the biggest H2 deliverables on this desk is the WhatsApp + Veta AI bot integrated with ezyVet — designed to deflect a meaningful share of routine call-centre volume across all brands. Once integrated, it materially reduces a recurring operating cost the company would otherwise carry indefinitely. This is owned, designed and largely already prototyped under the Marketing Director's portfolio.

05 · Combined functions, lean structure

A team where each role quietly covers the work of several.

Across the marketing department — the director and the wider team — most positions combine functions that elsewhere are split between multiple specialists. Brand, digital, performance, analytics, AI, finance and content responsibilities sit in fewer hands than the same scope would normally require. In the current period, where keeping spend disciplined matters, that compounded coverage represents a meaningful budget the company never has to spend.

06 · Tools, equipment, focused conditions

Working conditions that match the leverage of the role.

At this scope, every hour of focused strategic thinking has unusually high downstream leverage on commercial outcomes. Better equipment, working environment and the financial flexibility to delegate non-strategic personal load all translate directly into more of those high-leverage hours — and therefore better decisions, faster shipping, and stronger campaigns across all brands.

Reference · Compensation in the financial model The proposed Marketing Director compensation is already reflected in the Phase 1 and Phase 2 envelopes (see Financials tab). Both phases retain the 3.5% / 3.0% revenue ratios and preserve the reserved budget for new projects, events and capability investment — the review fits inside a budget that is itself reducing in absolute terms.
